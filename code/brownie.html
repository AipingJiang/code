Chocolate Brownie Cookies

Chocolate brownie cookies are a delightful hybrid of two beloved desserts, combining the rich, fudgy texture of brownies with the portable convenience of cookies. These cookies typically have a decadent, intensely chocolatey flavor and a soft, chewy interior, reminiscent of traditional brownies. The key ingredients include cocoa powder or melted chocolate, all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, brown sugar, eggs, butter, and a pinch of salt. Chocolate chips or chunks are often added to intensify the chocolate experience. The dough is scooped into cookie-sized portions and baked until they develop a slightly cracked surface but remain soft and fudgy on the inside. Chocolate brownie cookies offer the best of both worlds, making them a popular choice for chocolate enthusiasts who crave the taste of brownies in a more portable form.

Recipe

Ingredients

For the Cookie Dough:

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1 & 1/4 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips or chunks
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips or chunks (semi-sweet or milk chocolate)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 175Â°C and line one baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter and the chocolate chips (or chunks) together. You can do this in 20-30 second intervals, stirring between each interval, until the mixture is smooth. Alternatively, you can melt them in a heatproof bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler method).
  3. Once the chocolate and butter are melted and smooth, allow the mixture to cool for a few minutes.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. Slowly pour the melted chocolate mixture into the egg mixture, stirring continuously until well incorporated.
  6. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt (optional if using salted butter).
  7. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until just combined.
  8. Fold in the 1 cup of chocolate chips or chunks.
  9. Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop mounds of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving enough space between each for the cookies to spread.
  10. Bake for about 10-12 minutes, or until the cookies are set but still soft in the middle. The exact baking time can vary, so keep a close eye on them.
  11. Remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
