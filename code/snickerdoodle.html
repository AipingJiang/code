Snickerdoodles

Snickerdoodles are delightful round cookies with a soft, chewy texture, characterized by their pale golden-brown surface adorned with a sugary cinnamon coating. These classic treats boast a sweet and buttery flavor complemented by a distinct cinnamon warmth, thanks to the ground cinnamon and cream of tartar in the dough. Their preparation involves rolling the dough into small balls, then generously coating them in a sugar-cinnamon mix before baking, resulting in a cookie with a satisfying contrast of a crisp exterior and a tender, pillow-like center. Snickerdoodles have a storied history with probable Dutch or German roots, evolving into an enduring American favorite cherished for their timeless appeal, especially during the holiday season.

Recipe

Ingredients

For the Cookie Dough:

  • 2 & 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened (if using salted, skip the salt)
  • 1 & 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
For the Cinnamon Sugar Coating:
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 & 1/2 tablespoons ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 175Â°C and line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium-sized mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Set this dry mixture aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter and 1 1/2 cups of sugar together until the mixture becomes light and fluffy.
  4. Add the eggs and vanilla extract to the butter-sugar mixture and beat until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a smooth dough forms.
  6. In a small bowl, combine the remaining 1/4 cup of sugar and the ground cinnamon to make the cinnamon sugar coating.
  7. Use your hands to shape the cookie dough into 1-inch balls (you can use a cookie scoop for uniform sizes). Roll each ball in the cinnamon sugar mixture until it's coated evenly.
  8. Place the coated cookie dough balls onto the prepared baking sheets, leaving some space between each one.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes or until the edges of the cookies are lightly golden, but the centers are still soft. Be careful not to overbake.
  10. Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
