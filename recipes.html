Stuffed Mushrooms

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Makes: 16
Ingredients Quantity
Large white button mushrooms 16
Olive oil 2 tbsp
Garlic cloves 2, minced
Breadcrumbs 1/4 cup
Grated Parmesan cheese 1/4 cup
Fresh parsley, chopped 2 tbsp
Salt To taste

Step 1:Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it.

Step 2: Gently clean the mushrooms with a damp paper towel to remove any dirt. Remove the stems from the mushrooms and set them aside. Using a small spoon or a melon baller, carefully scoop out the center of each mushroom to create space for the stuffing. Be careful not to break the mushrooms.

Step 3: Finely chop the mushroom stems you set aside earlier.

Step 4: In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped mushroom stems. Cook for a few minutes until the garlic becomes fragrant and the mushroom stems are tender. Remove from heat.

Step 5: In a bowl, combine the cooked garlic and mushroom stems with breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix everything together until well combined.

Step 6: Using a small spoon, stuff each mushroom cap with the mixture, pressing it down gently to fill the cavity.

Step 7: Place the stuffed mushrooms on the prepared baking sheet.

Step 8: Bake in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes or until the mushrooms are cooked and the stuffing is golden brown.

Step 9: Remove from the oven and let the stuffed mushrooms cool slightly before serving.

Maldivian Tuna Curry

  • Time: 30 ~ 35 minutes
  • Serves: 4
\
Ingredients Quantity
Fresh tuna fillets 500g
Medium Onion 1, finely chopped
Garlic cloves 2, minced
Ginger 1-inch piece, grated
Green chilies 2-3, sliced (adjust to your preference)
Curry leaves 2-3
Tumeric powder 1 tsp
Chili powder 1 tsp (adjust to your preference)
Ground coriander 1 tsp
Ground cumin 1/2 tsp
Coconut milk 1 cup
Vegetable oil 2 tbsp
Salt To taste
Fresh coriander leaves For garnish
Cooked white rice For serving

Step 1: In a large pan or pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.

Step 2: Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, sliced green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the aromas release.

Step 3: Lower the heat and add the turmeric powder, chili powder, ground coriander, and ground cumin. Stir well, allowing the spices to coat the onion mixture evenly. If the mixture becomes too dry, you can add a splash of water to prevent burning.

Step 4: Add the bite-sized tuna pieces to the pan and gently stir to coat them with the spice mixture. Cook for a few minutes until the tuna starts to brown slightly.

Step 5: Pour in the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let the curry cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the tuna is cooked through and tender. Season with salt to taste.

Step 6: Once the curry is ready, remove it from the heat. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Step 7: Serve the Maldivian Tuna Curry hot over steamed white rice or with traditional flatbreads like "roshi."

Mango Coconut Panna Cotta

  • Time: 30 ~ 35 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Mango (or any other fruit) puree 1 cup
Coconut milk 1 cup
Heavy cream 1/2 cup
Granulated sugar 1/4 cup
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Gelatin powder 2 tsp
Water 1 tbsp
Fresh mango (or chosen fruit) slices For garnish (optional)
Shredded coconut For garnish (optional)

Step 1: In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin powder over the water and let it sit for about 5 minutes to bloom.

Step 2: In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, heavy cream, and granulated sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it begins to steam and the sugar dissolves. Do not let it boil.

Step 3: Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the bloomed gelatin. Mix well until the gelatin completely dissolves.

Step 4:Add the mango puree and vanilla extract to the coconut milk mixture, stirring until everything is well combined.

Step 5: Divide the mixture evenly into serving glasses or molds of your choice.

Step 6: Let the panna cotta cool to room temperature, then cover each glass with plastic wrap or a lid.

Step 7: Refrigerate the panna cotta for at least 4 hours or until it sets and becomes firm.

Step 8: Once the panna cotta is set, garnish with fresh mango slices and shredded coconut if desired.

Step 9: Serve chilled and enjoy the delightful flavors of Mango Coconut Panna Cotta!
